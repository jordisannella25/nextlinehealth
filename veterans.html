Why This Matters

Veteran behavioral health is not only a care issue. It is a continuity issue.

The VA reported 6,398 veteran suicides in 2023, averaging 17.5 veteran suicides per day. Many veterans also face behavioral health, substance use, housing, employment, and family stressors at the same time. Support cannot stop at a referral.

Veteran Support

A structured support layer for veterans after the first referral.

Many veterans are not missing support because resources do not exist. They are missing support because the system is fragmented, difficult to navigate, and inconsistent after the first point of contact.

NextLine Health closes that gap by helping veterans stay engaged, follow through on appointments, connect with appropriate resources, and remain accountable through consistent human contact.

We do not replace clinical care. We strengthen the path into care and support the continuity needed for better outcomes.

Why It Matters

The referral is only the beginning. The real challenge is staying connected.

Veterans often face stigma, paperwork, delays, transportation barriers, family pressure, work demands, and uncertainty about where to turn next. Without structured follow-through, even a strong referral can become another missed opportunity.

Engagement After Referral

02
